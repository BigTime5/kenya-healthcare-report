7. Key Insights & Policy Recommendations

ðŸŽ¯ Critical Insights

Insurance Coverage Crisis

Nearly 6 in 10 Kenyans lack health insurance, with coverage heavily skewed toward urban, employed, higher-income populations. The uninsured wait 2.4x longer between hospital visits, creating a two-tiered healthcare system.

Gender & Income Intersectionality

Low-income women face compounded healthcare disadvantages - 40% lower preventive care access, 65% lower cancer screening rates, and longer intervals between medical care compared to high-income men.

Geographic Healthcare Deserts

Northern Kenya exhibits healthcare apartheid - 18.4% insurance coverage vs. 68.3% in Nairobi, with hospital distances averaging 42.7km compared to 3.2km in urban centers.
